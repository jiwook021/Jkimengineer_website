Math prerequisites


First, learn basic arithmetic and algebra. Algebra is the study of mathematical symbols and the rules for manipulating them. Physics calculations require many algebraic manipulations and simple arithmetic calculations because most data is explained in math symbols and numbers. Each step needs to be mathematically proven to show accurate results and find meaningful property for various mechanical systems. After learning basic algebraic manipulation, learn how to graph any math equations. The relationship between variables needs to be graphed so that each system can be analyzed in various numbers.

Second, after learning introductory algebra, learn Calculus, a study of continuous change, to further explore physics equations. With Calculus, many mechanical relationships can be analyzed deeply with derivatives and integrals that cannot be explained with algebra. A derivative is a tool that measures the sensitivity to chance of a function value with respect to change, also called a slope. Integral express the area under the curve of a graph of the function. Derivative and integral is one of the fundamental principles that is needed to be mastered to understand the math relationships in Physics.
